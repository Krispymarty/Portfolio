# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is recruiters and engineering leads evaluating Yashmit Singh for internships and early-career AI/software roles. Secondary audiences are technical collaborators, hackathon teammates, and builders exploring AI product work. Visitors typically arrive with limited time and need to understand Yashmit's level, evidence, and trajectory quickly.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio turns a computer-science student's résumé and project record into an honest, memorable proof of growth from machine-learning experiments to usable AI systems. Success means a visitor can identify Yashmit's focus, inspect credible project evidence, and reach him without decoding a showcase experiment.

## Positioning

Yashmit connects model performance, explainability, and product engineering so AI work can move from notebooks into decisions people can understand and use.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: start a conversation by email.
- Secondary CTA: inspect the résumé, LinkedIn profile, or GitHub activity.
- Ten-second memory: "From model accuracy to useful decisions."
- Belief ladder: Yashmit understands ML foundations; he can measure and explain model behavior; he can connect models to APIs and product systems; he is actively growing into an AI product engineer.
- Proof on hand: `public/resume.pdf`, quantified fraud-detection and weapon-detection outcomes, NGO team leadership, education, GitHub activity, and the existing `public/profile.jpg` portrait.

## Brand Personality

Precise, curious, and kinetic. The tone is capable but student-appropriate: specific about demonstrated work, candid about work in progress, and free of inflated founder language.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic purple/blue developer glow, gaming interfaces, terminal cosplay, glass-card grids, decorative particles, fake dashboards, indistinguishable project cards, oversized gradient headings, and animation that delays access to content.

## Design Principles

1. Evidence before adjectives.
2. Make the progression from model to product visible.
3. Use spatial interaction to explain systems, never to obscure navigation.
4. Keep work-in-progress visibly distinct from verified outcomes.
5. Make every chapter useful without motion or 3D.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA where reasonably possible. Preserve semantic reading order, visible keyboard focus, a skip link, 44px touch targets, labelled controls, sufficient contrast, reduced-motion behavior, and a complete no-JavaScript/no-3D content path.
