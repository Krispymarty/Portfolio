# Portfolio purpose

## Primary goal

Present Yashmit Singh as a Computer Science (Data Science) student who can move from machine-learning evaluation and explainability into usable product systems. The portfolio must make technical evidence quick to inspect while demonstrating unusual frontend and 3D engineering craft.

## Target visitors

- Recruiters and engineering leads evaluating internship or early-career AI/software candidates.
- Technical collaborators and hackathon teammates.
- Builders interested in explainable AI, computer vision, APIs, and AI product systems.

## Five-second understanding

A visitor should understand that:

- this is Yashmit Singh;
- he is a computer-science student in Dehradun;
- he builds explainable AI and product systems;
- the strongest proof is in the selected projects;
- resume, projects, and contact are immediately available.

## Desired action

Recruiters should inspect one project, open the resume or GitHub profile, and start a conversation by email or LinkedIn. The immersive sequence is optional, not a gate.

## Emotional impression

Thoughtful, technically capable, curious, precise, crafted, and ambitious without exaggeration. The experience should feel like entering a real project workspace that becomes more complete as evidence accumulates.

## Differentiation

The portfolio connects three genuine strengths rather than presenting a skill inventory: measurable model work, explainability/decision thinking, and frontend/product engineering. Its low-poly workstation turns those relationships into a persistent visual system while the accessible HTML remains direct and recruiter-friendly.
